AIMS AND OBJECTIVES
  

(A) Tumour evolution using genomics  

  

Lecture: Introduction to cancer genome  (Prof Sarah McClelland) - 19 January 2026

  

 
Aim:  

To understand and gain knowledge of characteristics of cancer genome;  

To know common omics techniques to characterise cancer genome.  

 
Objectives:  

To understand the cancer genome  

To know the intrinsic characteristics of the cancer genome  

To know common omics techniques to study the cancer genome, and understand how the data is generated  

To know the basic analytic pipeline to analyse and visualise the data  

To know how the data is interpreted  

  

Lecture: Tumour evolution (Prof Sarah McClelland) - 19 January 2026

Aim:  

 
To understand the concept and consequences of tumour evolution;  

To know common techniques to study tumour evolution   

 
Objectives:  

To understand tumour evolution and its consequences  

To understand inter- and intro-tumour heterogeneity and implications  

To know how to study tumour evolution, common molecular techniques used.  

  

Lecture/Practical: Inference of tumour evolution using DNA sequencing (Prof Jun Wang) - 19 January 2026

  

 
Aim:  

 
To understand the techniques and technical details to study tumour evolution;    

To understand and practise the analytic workflow of sequencing data to infer tumour evolution  

Objectives:  

 
To understand the techniques and technical details of common sequencing studies on tumour evolution  

To learn and grasp the basic analytic workflow and key steps of such analysis  

To practise and infer tumour evolution based on sequencing data of single samples  

To practise and infer tumour evolution based on sequencing data of multiple samples, such as sequential and temporal samples   

To learn and know how to infer phylogenetic trees using mutations  

  

  

Lecture/Practical: Study tumour evolution using single cell sequencing  (Prof Sarah McClelland and Dr Molly Guscott) - 20 January 2026

  

Aim:  

To understand single cell DNA sequencing and applications in inferring tumour heterogeneity and evolution;  

To understand and practise the analytic workflow of single-cell sequencing data to infer tumour evolution  

Objectives:  

To understand single cell DNA sequencing and applications in tumour evolution  

To understand the data generated and how they can be used to infer tumour evolution  

To learn and practise the basic analytic workflow and key analytic steps  

To analyse and visualise single cell data to infer tumour heterogeneity and evolution   

   

(B) Tumour microenvironment and immunogenicity using omics  

  

Lecture: Tumour microenvironment  (Dr Oliver Pearce) - 23 January 2026

  

Aim:  

To understand tumour immune microenvironment and applications;  

To know common omics techniques to study tumour microenvironment  

Objectives:  

To understand tumour microenvironment and its consequences and applications in tumour diagnosis and prognosis  

To understand the concept of immunotherapy and response/resistance to immunotherapy  

To know how to study tumour microenvironment using common omics techniques  

  

  

  

Lecture/practical: Inference of immune microenvironment using RNA-seq (Prof Jun Wang) - 23 January 2026

  

Aim:  

 
To understand the techniques to deconvolute buik tissue RNA-seq to infer tumour microenvironment;  

To understand and practise the analytic workflow of the deconvolution techniques  

Objectives:  

To learn various analytic techniques to deconvolute tumour microenvironment using RNA-seq  

To understand and learn basic analytic workflow and key analytic steps  

To practise analytic workflow using RNA-seq of real world data  

To infer and compare tumour microenvironment across patient samples  

  

  

  

Lecture: Tumour immunogenicity  (Prof Yaohe Wang and Dr Louisa Chard) - 26 January 2026

  

Aim:  

 
To understand mechanisms of tumour immunogenicity;  

To understand neoantigens, and antigen presentation and processing machinery  

To understand the determinants of tumour immunogenicity  

 
Objectives:  

To learn and understand various mechanisms of tumour immunogenicity and potential consequences in patient survival  

To learn and understand what neoantigens are, and how they are presented by antigen presentation and processing machinery  

To learn and understand the defects of antigen presentation and processing machinery in tumour samples  

To learn and understand the determinants of tumour immunogenicity   

  

  

Lecture/Practical: Study tumour immunogenicity using omics data  (Prof Jun Wang)  - 26 January 2026

  

Aim:  

To understand how to infer tumour immunogenicity using omics data;  

To learn and practise analytic workflow to derive tumour neoantigens using sequencing data;  

  

Objectives:  

To learn various techniques to infer tumour immunogenicity using DNA sequencing data  

To learn and practise analytic workflows for HLA typing and neoantigen identification using DNA- and RNA-seq data  

To learn and practise how to identify aberrations in antigen presentation and processing machinery using omics data  

  

  

Lecture: co-evolution of cancer cells and immune microenvironment, their interaction (Prof Sarah McClelland) - 27 January 2026

  

Aim:  

To understand the interplay between cancer cells and tumour microenvironment;  

To understand how this interplay may affect the response/resistance to immunotherapy;  

To understand how this interplay shapes tumour and immune evolution  

  

Objectives:  

To learn the concept of the interplay between tumour and non-tumours cells, and determinants of this interplay  

To learn and understand how this interplay can be harnessed to improve the efficacy of anti-tumour therapies  

To learn and understand how this interplay shapes tumour evolution, and how this affect patients’ overall survival  

To learn and understand how this interplay influence the trajectory of immune response
