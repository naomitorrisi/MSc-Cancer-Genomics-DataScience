AIMS AND OBJECTIVES
  

(A) HPC and Bash Scripts  

  

Lecture/Practical: High-Performance Computing and Bash Scripts- 03 November 2025  

 
 
 
 
 
 
Aim:  

To understand and be able to use high-performance computing and bash script in the Linux system;  

To practise and consolidate the usage of HPC and bash scripts  

Objectives:  

To know what Linux system is and use Linux  

To know and use high-performance computing (HPC)  

To know and be able to submit jobs, use nodes and array jobs in HPC  

To know and be able to create simple bash scripts and some basic functions  

To practise the usage of HPC and writing simple bash scripts

(D) ChIP-seq and analytics  

  

Lecture/Practical: Regulatory elements, ChIP-seq and analysis - 10 November 2025  

 
Aim:  

To learn and understand regulatory elements, ChIP-seq and be able to analyse data generated from ChIP-seq;  

To practise and consolidate hands-on skills used for ChIP-seq data analysis   

 
Objectives:  

To know and understand what regulatory elements are, what ChIP-seq is and what questions it can address  

To understand basic analytic workflow and key analytic steps  

To know how to perform alignment and how to prepare the input file ready for peak calling  

To practise and perform ChIP-seq alignment, key steps before the peak calling, such as removing PCR duplicates and down sampling.  

  

Lecture/Practical: ChIP-seq peak calling and visualisation - 10 November 2025  

Aim:  

To understand and know how to perform ChIP-seq peak calling and other key processing steps;  

To practise and consolidate hands-on skills used for ChIP-seq peak calling and visualisation  

Objectives:  

To understand and learn peak calling analysis  

To understand and learn how to interpret the results and data filtering  

To understand and learn ways to visualise ChIP-seq results  

To practise and perform ChIP-seq peak calling analysis, peak filtering and visualisation  

  

  

Lecture/Practical: Peak annotation and motif analysis  - 10 November 2025 

Aim:  

To understand and know how to perform ChIP-seq peak annotation and motif analysis;  

To practise and consolidate hands-on skills used for ChIP-seq peak annotation and motif analysis    

 
Objectives:  

To understand and learn how to do peak annotation in relation to genes and regulatory elements  

To understand and learn how to perform motif enrichment analysis based on sequences captured by peaks  

To practise and perform peak annotation, visualise the distribution of reads in regions of interest, and motif analysis in the peak regions
