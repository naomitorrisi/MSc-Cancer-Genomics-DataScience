Aims and Objectives  
  

Lecture 1: Introduction to single cell omics  (02/03/2026) - Jessica Okosun

  

 
Aim:  

To introduce to students the basic knowledge of single cell omics and their applications in cancer research  

Objectives:  

To understand and grasp the knowledge and concept of single cell omics and relevant techniques  

To understand and grasp how single cell omics techniques are used in cancer research  

To understand and grasp what research questions can be addressed by single cell omics  

To understand and grasp the basic workflow of single cell experiments and analysis  

  

  

Lecture 2: Introduction to single cell RNA-seq analysis – single sample  (02/03/2026) - Mirjana Efremova

  

 
Aim:  

To introduce to students the analytics and workflow how to analyse single cell RNA-seq data of a single sample  

Objectives:  

To understand and grasp the overview and workflow of analysing single cell RNA-seq data of a single sample  

To know and understand the key analytic steps from raw input to explainable output  

To know and understand the tricks to visualise the single cell data  

To know and understand the analytics of identifying cell clusters and associated biological activities  

  

Practical 1: Single cell RNA-seq analysis – single sample  (03/03/2026) - Elise Smith, Ozen Leylek

  

Aim:  

To practise the analytics and workflow how to analyse single cell RNA-seq data of a single sample  

Objectives:  
 
To be able to analyse single cell RNA-seq data from raw input to meaningful output following the workflow  

To be able to perform normalisation, dimension reduction, and clustering  

To be able to derive enriched biological activities for identified clusters  

To be able to inspect the single cell RNA-seq data using feature plots
