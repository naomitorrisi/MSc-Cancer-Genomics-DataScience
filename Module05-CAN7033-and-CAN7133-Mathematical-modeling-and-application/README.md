Aims and Objectives  
  

Lecture: Introduction to mathematical modelling  (Benjamin Werner) - 10 February 2025

  

Aim:  

In this lecture we want to introduce some basic concepts of mathematical modelling in the natural sciences, starting from differential equation introduced by Newton going all the way to modern concepts of probabilistic theories and the emergence of complexity.    

Objectives:  

Introduction to basic mathematical concepts, e.g. differential equations (we will not study any mathematical aspects)  

Deterministic and Probabilistic modelling  

Modern approaches of causal networks  

The Moran process as an application  

The concept of emergence  

  

  

  

Lecture/Practical: Introduction to stochastic programming  (Nathaniel Mon Pere) - 10 February 2025

  

Aim:  

In all 4 practical sessions, we will work on problems of stochastic modelling by implementing certain mathematical models in Python.   

  

In the first section, we will introduce basic programming requirements.   

Objectives:  

  

Introducing and drawing random numbers from different probability distributions  

Plotting probability distributions  

Calculating mean and variances from a set of random numbers  

Program a 1-dimensional random walk and plot stochastic trajectories   

  

Lecture: Historic mathematical models in biology and cancer evolution  (Benjamin Werner) - 11 February 2025

  

Aim:  

Here we introduce three historically very important examples of mathematical models in biology and cancer evolution that helped our understanding of the underlying evolutionary forces:  

  

Objectives:  

  

Lotka-Volterra predator prey models  

The Luria-Delbrück experiment  

Armitage and Doll multi-stage theory of cancer evolution  

  

  

Lecture/Practical: Multi-stage process of cancer evolution  (Nathaniel Mon Pere) - 11 February 2025

  

Aim:  

Here we will revisit the famous Armitage and Doll model of carcinogenesis by programming the multi-stage process of cancer evolution  

  

Objectives:  

  

Introducing the concept of stochastic mutations  

Introducing single and multiple hit mutations  

Investigating stochastic trajectories of single and multiple hit mutations  

Large- population averages   

Waiting times to cancer and power law scaling  

 

 

  

Lecture: Somatic cancer evolution  (Benjamin Werner) - 17 February 2025

  

Aim:  

Here we introduce mathematical models of mutation accumulation. This directly leads to problems of intra-tumour genetic heterogeneity, inferences of evolution and selection in cancer  

  

Objectives:  

  

Recapitulating the Luria-Delbrück model  

Mathematical models of mutation accumulation and intra-tumour heterogeneity  

Aspects of neutral and positive selection in cancer genomes  

The odd case of healthy vs. cancerous tissues  

Modern tools to decipher cancer genomes  

  

  

Lecture/Practical: Introduction to the Moran Process  (Nathaniel Mon Pere) - 17 February 2025

  

Aim:  

Here we will introduce students to the stochastic Moran Process, one of the most fundamental frameworks in theoretical biology.   

  

Objectives:  

  

Introducing update rules of the Moran process  

Introducing Fixation probabilities and fixation times  

Introduction of selection  

Comparison to general birth death processes  

  

  

Lecture: The odd case of extrachromosomal DNA  (Benjamin Werner) - 18 February 2025

  

Aim:  

Here we take a peek into a current hot topic of cancer research and study some aspects of extrachromosomal DNA evolution in cancer genomes  

  

Objectives:  

  

Random vs. non-random segregation  

Rules of ecDNA inheritance and models of selection  

Time dynamics of ecDNA copies, single cell ecDNA intra-tumour heterogeneity  

Neutral evolution vs. selection  

Epistatic interactions and multi-species processes  

 

  

Lecture/Practical: Moran process in space  (Nathaniel Mon Pere) - 18 February 2025

  

Aim:  

Based on our previous lecture, we extend the Moran process to include spatial features. We will show, that sometimes, a spatial architecture can have a very strong influence on the evolutionary trajectories  

  

Objectives:  

  

Moran process on a line  

Moran process on a ring  

Comparison of fixation probabilities
